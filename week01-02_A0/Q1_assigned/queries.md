# Q1 — CO2 Concentration & Decision Quality (K-ATLAS Q16, EMPIRICAL ONLY)

## Question
At what CO2 concentration does strategic decision-making quality (measured by SMS or comparable instrument) begin to decline, and is the threshold lower for cognitively demanding vs. routine tasks?

**KA topic match:** CO2 Concentration × Decision Quality (Q16)
**Key instrument:** Strategic Management Simulation (SMS) — Streufert et al.
**Required article type:** Empirical experiments only (original data, human subjects, CO2 manipulation or measurement as IV, cognitive/decision outcome as DV)

## Search strategy

### Pattern A — Broad framing
1. What peer-reviewed evidence shows that elevated indoor CO2 concentrations impair human decision-making or cognitive performance in office or controlled laboratory settings, and at what concentration thresholds do effects become significant?

### Pattern B — Measure × condition × control
2. What do experimental studies using the Strategic Management Simulation (SMS) find when adult office workers are exposed to 1000 ppm or 2500 ppm CO2 compared to baseline 600 ppm conditions in terms of higher-order decision-making performance?
3. What experimental evidence shows that increasing indoor CO2 from 600 to 1000 ppm in office environments impairs complex cognitive tasks such as crisis response, strategic planning, or information usage compared to routine focused-activity tasks?
4. What do longitudinal observational studies find when measuring Stroop test or arithmetic throughput in office workers exposed to varying real-world CO2 concentrations across multiple countries and building types?

### Pattern C — Mechanism / type variants
5. What do controlled studies of chess tournament players or professional drivers exposed to elevated CO2 or particulate matter show about the causal impact of indoor air quality on real-world decision quality?
6. What evidence from randomized crossover trials shows that reducing CO2 via ventilation in school classrooms improves next-morning cognitive performance in children compared to high-CO2 sleep conditions?
7. What do astronaut-analog studies using the SMS and Cognition test batteries find when subjects are exposed to CO2 concentrations ranging from 0 to 1.2% in closed environments, and do results replicate the Satish 2012 threshold effect?

## Notes on what worked / didn't
- Google AI Citation: Good for finding foundational papers (Satish 2012, Allen 2016) but misses recent preprints and working papers
- PubMed PMC filter (free full text): Found Cedeño Laurent 2021 (PMC8942432), Bejder Klausen 2023 (PMC10464806), Young 2024 (PMC11221786)
- Europe PMC PDF render: Only reliable source for EHP papers (PMC download blocked by CAPTCHA); used for Satish 2012 (PMC3548274), Allen 2016 (PMC4892924), MacNaughton 2015 (PMC4661675)
- IZA Discussion Papers: Freely downloadable at docs.iza.org; Künn et al. 2019 DP12632
- Semantic Scholar API: Rate-limited (429 errors) — use with 3–5 s sleep between calls
