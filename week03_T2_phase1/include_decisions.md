# Phase 1 — Include Decisions

**Definition of Include for this corpus** (matches Track 2 quality bar):
- Peer-reviewed (or pre-print with ≥5 citations); empirical or systematic-review/meta-analysis
- Primary measurement OR explicit experimental manipulation of indoor CO₂, ventilation, PM2.5, thermal, or composite IEQ
- Cognition / decision-quality / productivity outcome (Stroop, SMS, n-back, GPT, cRAT, EEG correlate, error-rate proxy, etc.)
- Adult or school-age population in indoor setting (office, classroom, vehicle, closed-environment analog)
- Excludes: clinical CO₂-inhalation panic-induction studies (7.5 % breathing challenge), engineering-only HVAC papers without a behavioral DV, animal models without translational claim

**Target:** ≥8 Include decisions. **Achieved:** 12 new Include candidates beyond the existing 10 Q1-papers seed (= 22 total in working corpus before Phase 3 expansion).

## New Include candidates (Phase 1)

### I-01 · Herbig, Mayer, Norrefeldt, Wargocki — 2026 (in press)
- **Title:** Do carbon dioxide, volatile organic compounds and atmospheric pressure affect the cognitive performance of occupants in indoor environments? Results of a large-scale experiment with simulated flights.
- **Venue:** Int J Hyg Environ Health (2026, vol 275, art 114809)
- **DOI:** 10.1016/j.ijheh.2026.114809 · **PMID:** 42035679 · **Source query:** Q03, Q13, Q14
- **Cluster:** C1 (CO₂ × Decision Quality), with C3 spillover
- **Study type:** Large-scale chamber experiment with simulated flights; manipulates CO₂, VOC, atmospheric pressure as factorial.
- **Why Include:** *The single most important new find.* Wargocki + Herbig are the European canon; this is the most recent multi-factor experiment that disentangles CO₂ from VOCs from pressure — the exact confound that has dogged Satish 2012 / Allen 2016. Direct successor to the corpus. **High VOI.**

### I-02 · Yang, Dong, Wang et al. (Tianjin) — 2026
- **Title:** Impact of experimentally elevated CO₂ concentrations and temperature on cognitive function: An EEG-based study under constant ventilation.
- **Venue:** Ecotoxicol Environ Saf (2026, vol 309, art 119578)
- **DOI:** 10.1016/j.ecoenv.2025.119578 · **PMID:** 41601028 · **Source query:** Q03, Q09, Q11
- **Cluster:** C1 + C4 (cross-cluster)
- **Study type:** Within-subject experiment; EEG outcome under controlled CO₂ × temperature; constant-ventilation control.
- **Why Include:** Adds an objective neural correlate (EEG) to the SMS / Stroop behavioral DVs already in corpus. Constant-ventilation design isolates CO₂ from outdoor-air rate. Replication-relevant.

### I-03 · Ge, Tieskens, Vyas et al. (Boston U / BPS) — 2025
- **Title:** Decision tools for schools using continuous indoor air quality monitors: a case study of CO₂ in Boston Public Schools.
- **Venue:** Lancet Reg Health Am (2025, vol 48, art 101148)
- **DOI:** 10.1016/j.lana.2025.101148 · **PMID:** 40585557 · **Source query:** Q01, Q04, Q08
- **Cluster:** C3 (Ventilation × Office Productivity), C4 spillover
- **Study type:** Field deployment of 3 659 IAQ monitors across 125 buildings; co-designed visualisations; not a behavioral experiment.
- **Why Include:** Bridges the lab → field gap; directly uses the phrase "decision tools" — relevant to KA's argumentation layer. Useful as exemplar of how IAQ data feeds into operational decisions, even though the cognitive DV is indirect.

### I-04 · Dedesko, Pendleton, Young, Coull, Spengler, Allen — 2025
- **Title:** Associations between indoor air exposures and cognitive test scores among university students in classrooms with increased ventilation rates for COVID-19 risk management.
- **Venue:** J Expo Sci Environ Epidemiol (2025, vol 35, 661–671)
- **DOI:** 10.1038/s41370-025-00770-6 · **PMID:** 40204887 · **Source query:** Q03, Q08, Q11
- **Cluster:** C3 + C1
- **Study type:** Repeated-measures observational with policy-driven ventilation increase as natural experiment.
- **Why Include:** Allen/Spengler stream extension of MacNaughton 2015. Uses post-COVID ventilation upgrade as a quasi-experimental nudge — methodologically novel, directly downstream of the Satish/Allen lineage already in corpus.

### I-05 · Zhang, Ma, Zuoqiu (Sichuan U–Pittsburgh Inst.) — 2026
- **Title:** EEG response and productivity outcome under changing indoor environment.
- **Venue:** Front Public Health (2026, vol 14, art 1767357)
- **DOI:** 10.3389/fpubh.2026.1767357 · **PMID:** 41869617 · **Source query:** Q09
- **Cluster:** C4 (composite IEQ — temperature × acoustics × luminance)
- **Study type:** Within-subject 2×2×2 factorial in 30 students; CPI from EEG + Grooved Pegboard Test.
- **Why Include:** Although CO₂ is not the manipulated variable, the EEG-CPI methodology generalizes to C1 work; expands cluster C4 with an experimental rather than observational design.

### I-06 · Wälde, Amesberger, Würth, Reichert, Finkenzeller — 2026
- **Title:** Heat stress impacts affective processes and risk-taking behaviour.
- **Venue:** Sci Rep (2026, online ahead of print)
- **DOI:** 10.1038/s41598-026-47250-x · **PMID:** 41957074 · **Source query:** Q10
- **Cluster:** C4
- **Study type:** Between-subject (n=36); passive vs. combined vs. control heat stress; Balloon Analogue Risk Task and Beads Task as DVs.
- **Why Include:** Adds *risk-taking* and *belief-updating* (Beads Task) to the C4 cluster, which is otherwise dominated by attention/throughput DVs. Heat → decision-quality is a thinly-evidenced KA edge — the exact kind of high-VOI gap to fill.

### I-07 · Wang, Lin, Ptukhin, Liu (WPI / Northeastern) — 2024
- **Title:** Air quality in the car: How CO₂ and body odor affect drivers' cognition and driving performance?
- **Venue:** Sci Total Environ (2024, vol 911, art 168785)
- **DOI:** 10.1016/j.scitotenv.2023.168785 · **PMID:** 37996033 · **Source query:** Q03, Q15
- **Cluster:** C1 (vehicle-cabin extension)
- **Study type:** Within-subject (n=25); 3 CO₂ levels (800/1800/3500 ppm) × 2 odor conditions; highway driving simulator + working memory.
- **Why Include:** Replication candidate for Shimazaki 2025 (already in corpus) but with finer dose-response (3 levels vs. 2) and an interacting factor (odor). Cabin-air domain extension.

### I-08 · Herbig, Norrefeldt, Mayer et al. — 2023
- **Title:** Effects of increased recirculation air rate and aircraft cabin occupancy on passengers' health and well-being — Results from a randomized controlled trial.
- **Venue:** Environ Res (2023, vol 216 Pt 4, art 114770)
- **DOI:** 10.1016/j.envres.2022.114770 · **PMID:** 36370817 · **Source query:** Q14, Q15
- **Cluster:** C3 + C1
- **Study type:** RCT in simulated aircraft cabin; 2 recirculation rates × 2 occupancies.
- **Why Include:** Predecessor methodology to I-01; same Wargocki/Herbig group; provides the experimental scaffolding the new 2026 paper builds on. Helps reconstruct the research-program timeline for the Phase 5 report.

### I-09 · Zhao, Seow (NUS) — 2026
- **Title:** Environmental regulation, air pollution, and cognitive function among middle-aged and older adults in China: A quasi-experimental study.
- **Venue:** Ecotoxicol Environ Saf (2026, vol 309, art 119626)
- **DOI:** 10.1016/j.ecoenv.2025.119626 · **PMID:** 41512775 · **Source query:** Q05
- **Cluster:** C2 (PM2.5 × Cognition)
- **Study type:** Difference-in-differences design exploiting China's APPCAP regulation; 4-wave panel data (CHARLS).
- **Why Include:** Adds a population-scale, policy-instrumented identification strategy to the C2 cluster — complements the lab-scale Cedeño Laurent 2021 evidence with external validity. Useful for the contested-claim part of the argumentation layer.

### I-10 · Kuramochi, Tsurumi, Ishibashi — 2023
- **Title:** Meta-Analysis of the Effect of Ventilation on Intellectual Productivity.
- **Venue:** Int J Environ Res Public Health (2023, vol 20, art 5576)
- **DOI:** 10.3390/ijerph20085576 · **PMID:** 37107857 · **Source query:** Q17
- **Cluster:** C3
- **Study type:** Systematic review + meta-analysis of ventilation × productivity studies.
- **Why Include:** Highest-leverage entry point into the cluster — provides a pooled effect size and a curated bibliography that will feed Phase 4 scale-up. Also a methodological model for the Phase 5 report.

### I-11 · Solid-fuel SR/MA — 2025
- **Title:** The association between indoor air pollution from solid fuels and cognitive impairment: a systematic review and meta-analysis.
- **Venue:** Rev Environ Health (2025)
- **DOI:** 10.1515/reveh-2023-0158 · **PMID:** 38413202 · **Source query:** Q17
- **Cluster:** C2 (broader IAQ pollutant)
- **Study type:** SR + meta-analysis.
- **Why Include:** Out-of-population (low-/middle-income contexts; older adults; biomass cooking) but provides effect-size benchmarks for *non-CO₂* IAQ → cognition. Useful boundary case for the K-ATLAS Q16 generalization argument.

### I-12 · CO₂ in spaceflight — review 2025
- **Title:** Carbon Dioxide as a Multisystem Threat in Long Duration Spaceflight.
- **Venue:** Aerosp Med Hum Perform (2025)
- **DOI:** 10.3357/AMHP.6716.2025 · **PMID:** 41253359 · **Source query:** Q16
- **Cluster:** C1 (high-end of dose-response, closed-environment)
- **Study type:** Narrative / scoping review.
- **Why Include:** Provides the closed-environment / chronic-exposure ceiling on the dose-response curve; complements Scully 2019 (already in corpus) and the HDT-bedrest studies (PMID 33630672, 34512371).
