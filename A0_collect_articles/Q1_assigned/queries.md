# Q1 — Biophilic design & stress physiology (EMPIRICAL ONLY)

## Question
What do controlled experimental studies measuring salivary cortisol, heart-rate variability, or skin conductance find when healthy adults are exposed to indoor environments containing natural elements (potted plants, living walls, natural materials, or views of greenery) compared to plant-free control environments, and do these physiological results support Stress Recovery Theory's prediction that biophilic features accelerate autonomic recovery?

**Theory anchor:** Stress Recovery Theory (Ulrich, 1983)
**Required article type:** Empirical experiments only — RCT, within-subjects, quasi-experimental lab/field
