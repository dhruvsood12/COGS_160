# Q2 — Circadian-effective lighting & cognitive performance (ANY article type)

## Question
What peer-reviewed evidence shows that variations in circadian-effective lighting — melanopic lux, blue-enriched white light, or dynamic day-like lighting schedules — in office and classroom settings affect alertness, sustained attention, and working-memory performance in adults, and what mechanisms involving melanopsin-mediated ipRGC signalling to the suprachiasmatic nucleus explain these effects?

**KA topic match:** Luminous Environment × Cognitive Performance (Circadian), VOI 0.57, *developing*
**Theory anchor:** ipRGC/melanopsin → SCN alerting pathway (CIE S 026, Lucas et al. 2014)
**Required article type:** Any — experiments, reviews, meta-analyses, theoretical, position papers all count for Q2.

### Primary (Pattern A, broad)
1. What peer-reviewed evidence shows that blue-enriched or high-melanopic white light in offices improves alertness, subjective sleepiness, and working-memory performance in adult workers compared with standard warm-white lighting, and does melanopsin-mediated ipRGC signalling explain these alerting effects?

### Variants
2. What experimental studies measuring psychomotor vigilance (PVT), Karolinska Sleepiness Scale, and n-back working memory performance find when office workers are exposed to high melanopic EDI lighting during the workday compared to low melanopic control lighting?
3. What systematic reviews and meta-analyses of dynamic or tunable LED lighting in workplaces report about effect sizes on cognitive performance, mood, and sleep quality in healthy adults?
4. What classroom studies of daylight quantity or dynamic electric lighting report about student attention, reading fluency, and mathematics performance, and how do these findings relate to circadian entrainment mechanisms?
5. What neuroimaging and psychophysiological evidence links melanopsin-containing intrinsically photosensitive retinal ganglion cells (ipRGCs) to non-visual alerting responses via the suprachiasmatic nucleus and locus coeruleus?
6. What recommendations do the CIE S 026 standard and Brown et al. (2022) consensus statement make about melanopic equivalent daylight illuminance (mEDI) thresholds for daytime cognitive performance and evening sleep preservation?
